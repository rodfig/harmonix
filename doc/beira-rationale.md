🍶 The structural problem of pairing kaiseki

Kaiseki cuisine is built on:

subtle umami

low fat

temperature variation

texture more than intensity

sequential progression (light → deeper → clean finish)

Most wine regions fail here because they produce wines that are:

too powerful
too alcoholic
too tannic
too oak-driven

⭐ Beira Interior (the surprise)

Because of:

altitude (500–700 m)

granite soils

continental nights

lighter extraction traditions

forgotten varieties (Rufete, Marufo, Síria)

you get wines that are:

fresh but not sharp
light but not dilute
textural without weight
aromatic without oak dominance

This is extremely rare globally.
