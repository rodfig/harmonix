
Validation files serve as diagnostic reports and are never edited directly.

All corrections occur in source producer files.

# 6. Inclusion Philosophy

Producers were selected according to behavioural contribution rather than fame.

A producer was included when it introduced:

- a new structural behaviour,
- a missing regional expression,
- or statistical realism.

This led to intentional inclusion of:

- cooperatives,
- everyday wines,
- historically representative producers.

Conversely, some prestigious producers were excluded when their wines duplicated already represented behaviours.

---

# 7. Panorama Analysis

The panorama system evaluates the database as a whole using:

- structure signatures,
- clustering by sensory structure,
- anchor wines,
- outliers,
- cluster absorption.

## Key Concepts

### Structural Novelty Rate (SNR)
Measures how often new wines introduce previously unseen structures.

### Cluster Absorption
Indicates whether new wines expand the model or reinforce existing patterns.

### Behavioural Poles
Extremes that define stylistic boundaries within Portuguese wine.

# 8. Regional Development

Regions were developed progressively:

1. **Discovery phase** — extremes identified.
2. **Expansion phase** — missing behaviours added.
3. **Integration phase** — bridge wines introduced.
4. **Stabilization phase** — everyday wines calibrated averages.
5. **Closure phase** — additions ceased once novelty diminished.

Alentejo served as a primary case study of this process.

## 9.1 Portugal Is Not Structurally North vs South

The dominant organizing principle is freshness versus density rather than geography.

## 9.2 Dão as Structural Hinge

Dão wines consistently bridge multiple clusters, connecting Atlantic and continental expressions.

## 9.3 Importance of Everyday Wines

Cooperative and mid-tier wines stabilize national structure and prevent prestige bias.

## 9.4 Talha Wines Integrate Naturally

Traditional talha wines form a textural corridor rather than isolated anomalies.

# 10. Limitations

- Sensory assignments involve informed interpretation.
- Technical sheets vary in completeness.
- Vintage evolution is not modeled.
- Some producers lack usable published data.
- The dataset represents behaviour, not market share.

# 11. Region Closure Criteria

A region is considered complete when:

- Structural Novelty Rate falls below ~0.10.
- New wines are absorbed into existing clusters.
- No behavioural pole remains unrepresented.

Using these criteria, Alentejo reached closure during the final development phase.

The Portuguese Wine Database demonstrates that national wine identity can be understood through structure and behaviour rather than geography alone.

# 15. The Database as an Analytical Object

This section examines the Portuguese Wine Database not as a construction process, but as a completed analytical system.  
Rather than describing methodology, the goal here is to understand **what the database itself reveals** once assembled.

---

## 15.1 Scale and Structural Density

At the conclusion of the current development phase, the database contains:

- 447 wines
- 14 Portuguese regions
- more than 100 unique structural signatures

A *structure signature* represents a unique combination of:

- perceived acidity
- tannin level
- body
- sweetness
- finish persistence.

The number of signatures relative to total wines indicates **high structural density**.  
No single wine type dominates the system, and diversity emerges organically rather than through forced categorization.

The largest recurring structure represents only a small fraction of total wines, demonstrating that Portuguese wine identity is plural rather than monolithic.

---

## 15.2 Distribution of Wine Types

Analysis of the completed dataset shows several important balances:

### Colour and Style
- Red wines form the structural backbone of the system.
- White wines provide the highest structural diversity.
- Fortified wines define extreme boundaries rather than central tendencies.

### Structural Persistence
A notable property of the dataset is the dominance of wines with **long finishes**.  
Persistence appears as a recurring national trait across multiple regions.

This suggests that Portuguese wine identity is strongly linked to gastronomic compatibility rather than immediacy or aromatic intensity alone.

---

## 15.3 Structural Continuum

One of the clearest emergent properties is the existence of a continuous national gradient:


Regions do not appear as isolated stylistic islands.  
Instead, wines transition gradually across structural space.

Examples include:

- Vinho Verde and Atlantic whites anchoring freshness,
- Dão and Beira Interior forming equilibrium zones,
- Alentejo expressing density while maintaining structural continuity.

This continuum contradicts simplified geographic narratives of Portuguese wine.

---

# 16. Emergent Structural Findings

The most significant outcomes of the project were not predefined.  
They emerged only after sufficient structural coverage was achieved.

---

## 16.1 Portugal Organizes by Structure, Not Region

Clustering analysis demonstrates that wines group primarily according to structural behaviour rather than appellation.

Wines from different regions frequently occupy identical clusters, while wines from the same region may span multiple structural zones.

This indicates that Portuguese wine identity is better understood as a **shared structural language** rather than a set of isolated regional styles.

---

## 16.2 The Central Role of Gastronomic Whites

The most recurrent structural profile in the database is:

- dry
- medium body
- elevated perceived acidity
- long finish.

These wines appear across regions including Dão, Bairrada, Lisboa, and parts of Alentejo.

The finding suggests that Portugal’s strongest national signature lies not in powerful reds but in **high-freshness, food-oriented white wines**.

---

## 16.3 Dão as Structural Hinge

Dão occupies a unique position within the structural map.

Its wines repeatedly bridge:

- Atlantic freshness,
- continental equilibrium,
- and southern density.

Removing Dão wines from the dataset would fragment cluster continuity, demonstrating that the region functions as a structural mediator within Portuguese wine.

This role emerges analytically rather than historically.

---

## 16.4 The Importance of Everyday Wines

Early versions of the dataset were unintentionally biased toward prestige producers.

The introduction of cooperative and mid-tier wines produced a measurable effect:

- cluster centers stabilized,
- structural extremes lost dominance,
- national averages became realistic.

This demonstrates that everyday wines are essential to accurately modelling a wine culture.

---

## 16.5 Integration of Traditional Methods

Talha wines initially appeared as stylistic outliers.  
As coverage increased, they formed a coherent textural corridor within existing clusters.

Rather than representing anomalies, traditional methods integrate naturally into Portuguese structural diversity.

This challenges the perception of talha wines as purely experimental or niche.

---

# 17. Regional Evolution as Model Development

Understanding evolved progressively during construction.

### Phase 1 — Discovery
Extreme wines defined structural boundaries.

### Phase 2 — Expansion
Missing stylistic behaviours were identified and added.

### Phase 3 — Bridging
Intermediate wines connected isolated clusters.

### Phase 4 — Stabilization
Everyday wines corrected prestige bias.

### Phase 5 — Closure
Regions ceased to produce new structural information.

Alentejo represents the clearest example of this progression, evolving from a region defined by powerful reds into a complete behavioural spectrum.

---

# 18. Structural Properties of Portuguese Wine

Across the entire dataset, several national characteristics emerge:

1. **Balance over excess** — even powerful wines retain structural freshness.
2. **Persistence as identity** — long finishes recur across regions.
3. **Continuity over contrast** — stylistic transitions are gradual.
4. **Multiplicity within unity** — diversity exists without fragmentation.

Portugal appears less as a collection of styles and more as a connected ecosystem.

---

# 19. Limits of the Model

The database intentionally excludes certain dimensions:

- vintage evolution,
- market representation,
- production volume,
- pricing hierarchy.

These omissions are deliberate, allowing structure to remain the primary analytical axis.

Interpretations should therefore be understood as structural rather than economic or historical conclusions.

---

# 20. Interpretation

The completed database suggests that Portuguese wine cannot be adequately described through regional stereotypes alone.

Instead, it behaves as a continuous structural landscape shaped by climate gradients, historical practices, and gastronomic orientation.

The database therefore functions as a **model of behaviour**, revealing relationships that are difficult to observe through traditional classification systems.

---

# 21. Significance

The project demonstrates that a national wine identity can emerge from aggregated sensory structure when:

- schema consistency is maintained,
- inference is minimized,
- and inclusion is guided by behavioural contribution.

The Portuguese Wine Database should thus be understood not merely as documentation, but as an analytical framework capable of describing how Portuguese wine behaves as a system.
