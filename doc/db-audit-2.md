## 1. Structural Diversity

### Result: High behavioural richness

Key indicators:

- 112 unique structure signatures
- Largest signature represents only **10.5%** of wines
- 46 signatures occur only once

### Interpretation

The database is not dominated by repetition.  
Instead, it spans a wide behavioural space.

This indicates the project has moved beyond cataloguing producers and now models **wine behaviour diversity**.

---

## 2. The Structural Center of Portuguese Wine

The most frequent behaviour observed:

- White wines
- Medium body
- Dry
- Long finish
- Acidity 7–8

This profile corresponds to Portugal’s modern gastronomic white wine identity.

### Meaning

The database correctly identifies the national stylistic attractor:

> structured, high-acid, food-oriented dry whites.

This is not bias but an emergent property of the dataset.

---

## 3. Behavioural Families (Clusters)

Although clustering produced 14 technical clusters, they collapse into approximately **five macro behavioural families**:

| Behaviour Family | Description |
|---|---|
| Atlantic structured whites | high-acid gastronomic whites |
| Balanced core wines | medium structure across colours |
| Structured reds | tannic, long-finish reds |
| Rich / powerful wines | warm-climate and fortified-adjacent |
| Oxidative / powerful whites | long-finish or evolved profiles |

These families represent roughly two-thirds of the dataset.

### Interpretation

Portuguese wine appears structurally coherent rather than fragmented.

---

## 4. Fortified Wine Integration

Fortified wines form a clear behavioural cluster:

- sweet
- medium–full body
- long finish

Importantly, they connect structurally to rich dry wines rather than forming isolated anomalies.

### Meaning

The structural scale functions consistently across categories — a major validation of schema design.

---

## 5. Rosé Representation — Identified Gap

- Rosé wines: **18 / 408 (~4.4%)**

Current representation mostly overlaps with light white behaviour.

Underrepresented styles:

- gastronomic rosé
- structured southern rosé
- oxidative or skin-contact rosé

### Implication

Rosé diversity is a clear opportunity for future expansion.

---

## 6. Outliers and Innovation Sources

Extreme or rare signatures concentrate in:

- Açores
- Madeira
- Beira Interior
- Trás-os-Montes

Examples include:

- lowest pH wines
- highest structural acidity
- rare structural combinations

### Interpretation

Peripheral regions act as **innovation zones**, expanding behavioural boundaries.

---

## 7. Sensory Signal Density

Median values:

- Aroma descriptors: ~4 per wine
- Pairing affinities: ~5 per wine

### Assessment

Signal density is well balanced:

- sufficient semantic richness
- minimal descriptive noise

The dataset avoids encyclopedic inflation.

## 8. Transformation of the Database

Although organized by producer files, semantic analysis shows:

- clustering ignores producers
- behaviour dominates organization

The project has effectively transitioned into a:

> **behavioural model of Portuguese wine**

rather than a producer archive.

## 10. Strategic Implication

The database already provides a credible structural panorama of Portuguese wine.

Future progress should focus on **edge expansion**, not volume growth.

Recommended targets:

- warm-climate elegance (southern regions)
- structured rosé styles
- lower-acid plush reds
- oxidative dry whites

---

## Final Conclusion

The Portuguese Wine Database has successfully crossed from construction into modelling.

The semantic audit demonstrates that:

- Portuguese wine behaviour emerges naturally from the data
- no single region dominates excessively
- diversity arises from structure rather than producer count

The project now operates as a coherent representation of Portuguese wine behaviour space.
